Description

“Civic” – (adj) of, or pertaining to a city, citizens, citizenship; e.g. civic engagement “Hacking” – (v) activity done by “hackers,” usually meaning computer related, but generalized to people who reuse, repurpose or bend processes, tools, etc to be used for other means; e.g. hacking the system

Technology is changing how people interact with the environment and how we understand the environment itself. Social Media, cell phones, social networking, crowdsourcing, apps, and other Web tools all provide a window into how people move, interact, feel, and even seen spaces. These tools, the Open Data Movement, and Open Source Culture have started to bridge the worlds of civic engagement and technology with cities, city agencies, non-profits, universities and companies hosting hackathons, app competitions, and meetups. Fundamentally this technology changes the relationship between citizen and city, reinvigorating conversations about power, access and democracy.

Civic Hacking is not necessarily contained to the digital realm. New resources and information available online are providing citizens tools for better understanding how the city works. With the information, data, and tools to empower and organize themselves, citizens are creating non-digital hacks on the city such as temporary uses for vacant lots, protest weddings, and urban farms.

As future planners, architects, urban designers, public health professionals, public administrators or even simply engaged citizens, you will be part of bringing together the world of technology with urban spaces and designing new tools for community participation in the planning process. With your communities as your users, we will focus on using the Human-Centered Design (HCD) process in developing our ideas for these new tools.

This workshop class evolved from the Crowd Sourced City Class (FA10, FA11) and will explore the tools available for civic hacking in real life applications and focus on using the human-centered design process to change the future of participatory planning.

The main objectives of the course will be to:

  1. gain a solid background in civic technology and the human-centered design process
  2. gain hands on experience with civic organizations
  3. prototype and implement actual civic hacking strategies and critique results.

Class Format

Class sessions will be a mixture of discussions, speakers, and workshops/hack sessions. The workshops are meant to extend the knowledge of technology for each student and explore ideas for implementation. Class will sometimes be exchanged for engaging with the civic technology community at events/conferences.

FAQs

Do I need to know GIS or programming to take this class?

There are no pre-reqs for this class. You do not need to know GIS nor any sort of programming for this class. It is important though that you are willing to look at code and try to work with it.

Do I need a Website?

Depending on the projects you chose to take on, students may need to purchase web hosting and/or a website URL. This can be done as a group of students or individually.

Do I need a smart phone?

Smart Phones are not required for the class, although some activities will be easier with a web-enabled phone. We will also specifically be talking about tools that do not require smart phones as many citizens do not have smart phones or data plans.
